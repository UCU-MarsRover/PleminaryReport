The navigation sensor suite is designed to provide full \ang{360} perception, enabling simultaneous state estimation and collision avoidance.

A StereoLabs ZED 2i stereo camera is mounted at the front of the rover, providing RGB imagery and depth data at \numproduct{1280 x 720} resolution and \qty{60}{\fps}. The wide \ang{110}~$\times$~\ang{70} (H$\times$V) FoV ensures detection coverage beyond the rover's direct forward path. Depth perception is effective within a range of \qtyrange{0.3}{20}{\metre}. The camera integrates a 6-axis IMU operating at \qty{400}{\hertz} and a magnetometer, providing inertial measurements for state estimation.

An RPLIDAR S2 is mounted on top of the rover, delivering a \ang{360} 2D depth scan with \ang{0.12} angular resolution at \qty{10}{\hertz}. The sensor reliably detects obstacles up to \qty{10}{\metre} regardless of surface reflectivity and lighting conditions, enabling detection of rover-scale obstacles and large terrain features.

Three Sipeed Maix Sense A010 ToF cameras mounted on the left flank, right flank, and rear collectively provide \ang{210} non-forward coverage. Each camera delivers a \numproduct{100 x 100} depth map at up to \qty{20}{\fps} with a \ang{70}~$\times$~\ang{60} (H$\times$V) FoV. The effective sensing range of \qtyrange{0.2}{2.5}{\metre} enables detection of close-range obstacles outside the stereo camera and LiDAR coverage zones, though performance may degrade under direct bright sunlight, with the RPLidar S2 maintaining omnidirectional coverage in such conditions.

The ZED 2i and RPLidar S2 jointly provide input to state estimation and localization (\autoref{par:state_estimation_localization}), while all sensors contribute to collision avoidance (\autoref{par:autonomous_navigation_collision_avoidance}).
