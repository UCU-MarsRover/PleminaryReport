Mars Yard conditions were characterised based on ERC rulebook specifications and historical event data. The terrain consists of dry sand and regolith simulant, generating significant airborne dust during rover operation. September weather in Kielce, Poland presents a realistic probability of moderate rainfall, which combined with sandy terrain creates localised mud conditions. Based on this analysis, IP54 was selected as the design reference standard: the "5" rating addresses dust exposure during normal operation, and the "4" rating addresses omnidirectional rain splash without pressurised jets.
