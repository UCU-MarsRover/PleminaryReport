\section{RIO Analysis}
\label{sec:rio}

\subsection{Methodology}
\label{subsec:rio_method}

The risk identification and assessment process follows a standard qualitative
risk management approach. Each risk is characterised by a \textbf{Likelihood}
(L) and a \textbf{Severity} (S), both scored on a 1--5 scale (Table~\ref{tab:rio_scales}).
The \textbf{Risk Index} (RI) is defined as:
\[
    \mathrm{RI} = L \times S
\]
Based on RI, risks are classified as:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item \riskhigh{} \quad RI $\geq$ 15
    \item \riskmedium{} \quad 6 $\leq$ RI $<$ 15
    \item \risklow{} \quad RI $<$ 6
\end{itemize}

Response strategies used: \textit{Accept}, \textit{Mitigate},
\textit{Transfer}, \textit{Avoid}.
