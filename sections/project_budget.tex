\subsection{Budget Analysis}
\label{subsec:budget_analysis}

The project budget is structured with a high level of granularity and transparency, 
providing a detailed breakdown across all major subsystems, including rover hardware, 
drone hardware, and non-engineering costs. Each subsystem is decomposed into individual 
components with clearly specified unit costs, quantities, and total costs. This level of 
detail allows precise tracking of expenditures and ensures full traceability of every 
budget allocation decision.

The rover hardware constitutes the dominant portion of the budget, reflecting the 
system's complexity and central role in mission execution. The allocation prioritises 
critical subsystems such as actuation (motors and reducers), perception (LiDAR, cameras, 
ToF sensors), and onboard computing (NVIDIA Orin NX), which are essential for autonomy 
and reliable operation. The drone subsystem, while significantly smaller in scale, is 
carefully optimised to provide complementary aerial capabilities with a minimal but 
sufficient set of components.

The budget demonstrates a strong balance between commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) 
components and cost-efficient engineering decisions. Wherever reliability, availability, 
and performance are critical, COTS solutions are selected (e.g., flight controllers, 
motors, embedded computers, and communication modules). This reduces development risk 
and ensures compatibility with existing ecosystems. At the same time, cost efficiency 
is achieved by avoiding unnecessary over-engineering and selecting components that 
provide the best price-to-performance ratio.

Non-engineering costs are explicitly separated and carefully estimated based on team size, 
duration of stay, and logistics requirements. The inclusion of detailed travel, accommodation, 
and operational expenses reflects realistic planning and ensures that the project budget 
accounts for all aspects of competition participation, not only technical development.

Finally, the inclusion of a contingency reserve introduces robustness to the financial plan, 
allowing the team to absorb unforeseen expenses such as component replacement, price 
fluctuations, or logistical adjustments.
